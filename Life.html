Timeline of Steve Job's Life

Date Major Event/Accomplishment
February 24th, 1955 Steve is born and quickly adopted by the Jobs Family
Summer 1968 Steve Jobs gets a summer job at the HP factory
1969 Steve Jobs meets Steve Wozniak and they become good friends
1973 Steve spends the fall semester at Reed College in Oregon, then drops out
March 1976 Woz and Steve show the early Apple I board at the Homebrew Computer Club
April 1976 Apple Computer Inc. is incorporated by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak and Ron Wayne
1978 Steve's ex-girlfriend Chris-Ann Brennan gives birth to their daugher Lisa. Steve refuses to acknowledge he is the father
Early 1981 Steve kicks Jef Raskin off of the Macintosh project and takes over
Summer 1985 The Pixar team is first introduced to Steve
September 17 1985 Steve Jobs resigns from Apple and starts NeXT with five other refugees from Apple.
April 1989 Steve Jobs is named 'Entrepreneur of the decade' by Inc. magazine
March 1991 Steve Jobs marries Laurene Powell
Fall 1991 Laurene gives birth to Steve’s first son, Reed Paul Jobs
March 1993 Steve's Father passes away
November 1995 One week after Toy Story is out, Steve Jobs's worth rises to $1.5 billion, more than it ever was at Apple
Late 1995 Laurene gives birth to Erin Siena Jobs, Steve's third child
August 1997 Steve Jobs introduces Apple's new board of directors and a truce with Microsoft at Macworld Boston
May 1998 Eve Jobs, Laurene and Steve's youngest daughter, is born
October 2001 Steve Jobs unveils iPod at a small media event on the company's campus
Spring 2003 Steve Jobs announces that Pixar is seeking a new distributor to replace Disney after its contract expires
Fall 2003 Steve Jobs is diagnosed with pancreatic cancer, but stubbornly refuses any modern medical treatment for months
August 2004 Steve has his tumor removed through surgery
January 2006 Steve Jobs unveils the first two Intel Macs at Macworld, the iMac and the new MacBook Pro
June 2007 iPhone is released in the US, the same day as Pixar’s 8th feature film, Ratatouille
January 2009 Steve Jobs announces he will not speak at Macworld 2009 because of his health, and takes a six-month medical leave of absence
April 2009 Steve is weeks away from dying, and therefore receives a liver transplant in Tennessee
January 2010 Steve Jobs unveils iPad, 'the biggest thing Apple's ever done'
January 2011 Steve takes a surprising leave of medical absence, without announcing an end date
March 2011 Although he is still on his medical leave, Steve announces the IPad 2
June 2011 Steve Jobs appears at the Cupertino City Council to unveil Apple's new plans and this is his last public appearance
August 2011 Steve resigns as Apple CEO
October 2011 Steve passes away at home, surrounded by family, at the age of 56
